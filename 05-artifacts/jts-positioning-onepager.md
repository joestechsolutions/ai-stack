## Three positioning tracks

### Track A — **PRIMARY** — JTS as a boutique AI consultancy for small business

**The pitch:** "You need a custom AI system but you're not going to hire an AI team. JTS builds it, runs it, hands you the keys."

**Who it's for:** Local / small businesses with a real workflow problem that AI solves cleanly. They have revenue, they have pain, they don't have technical staff. Think salons, contractors, niche e-commerce, professional services.

**The pattern (already proven with two live clients):**
- **Van (salon app, in build):** Custom app for an Archive hair salon — Expo RN, real business workflow, replacing off-the-shelf tools that don't fit. Not "an AI chatbot." A real business system.
- **Zach (Hostinger KVM ops, $8/mo recurring):** Operational automation that keeps the lights on without Zach touching the server. Cheap to deliver, recurring to JTS. The textbook retainer pattern.

**Why this is the primary track:**
1. Joe already has the proof — two live clients on the same model
2. Sales cycle is short — small biz owners make fast decisions
3. The 16-agent Hermes stack is the *tool*, not the *product* — the product is "your custom system, built and run"
4. Aligns with Joe's actual strengths: hardware-software bridge, ships-and-adapts, persistence
5. The AV pedigree (Waymo, Uber ATG) is the credibility floor — no small biz owner Googles "is Joe legit" and finds anything but wins

**The trap to avoid:** selling "AI strategy" or "AI workshops" or "AI audits." That's agency-speak. Joe builds. He ships. He runs it. The product is the system.

## Case studies (drop-in, one paragraph each)

### Van — Archive Hair Salon App

A Sacramento hair salon needed a custom app to run the parts of their business that off-the-shelf salon software couldn't touch — intake, archive lookups, a workflow that fit how the salon actually works, not how a SaaS product manager imagined salons work. JTS designed and is building the Expo React Native app with Stripe Connect for payments, real-time data sync, and a client experience that doesn't feel like enterprise software. Live with the salon owner, shipping in iterations, replacing a stack she was duct-taping together with paper, a Squarespace site, and Venmo. **Custom app. Real business. No off-the-shelf compromise.**

### Zach — Hostinger KVM Operational Automation

A small e-commerce operator was paying $8/month for a Hostinger KVM but spending hours a month on manual ops — security patching, monitoring, restart-on-fail, log review. JTS built a minimal-ops automation layer (cron, systemd, scripted health checks, alerting via the channels he already checks) that turned the server from a pet into cattle he doesn't have to feed. Recurring monthly engagement — the customer never thinks about the server, the server never goes down quietly, and JTS gets a small predictable retainer. **$8 of infra. Recurring margin. Pattern: take a job someone hates doing, do it for them, charge for the relief.**

## The 22-week AI Engineering Roadmap — verdict

**The roadmap that was built for Joe is a beginner AI curriculum. It does not match who Joe is.** It assumed "Python comfortable, AI/ML new" and produced a 22-week ladder that starts with "what is an LLM" and ends with "first deployed app." Joe has 16 agents in production, a multicloud Terraform footprint, a real RN+Stripe app in a real salon's hands, and 15 years of shipping physical systems at companies that don't give out logos. He doesn't need a Karpathy hour. He needs **targeted gap-filling in (a) the specific framework or vendor certifications that close a deal (LangChain cert, GCP pro, AWS SA-A if a job comes up), (b) the small set of patterns he hasn't hit yet (eval harnesses, observability at scale, model-routing at the edge), and (c) a Go-To-Market playbook for JTS-as-consultancy that's grounded in his actual wins, not a generic "AI services" template.** Recommend: **scrap the 22-week roadmap, rebuild in one weekend as a 4-week gap-fill plan, and start using the time saved for outreach.** The 22 weeks are not the asset. The 16 agents in prod are the asset. Spend the time on those.
